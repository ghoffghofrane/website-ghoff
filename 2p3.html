Introduction to Palestine

Palestine is a geographic region in Western Asia that has been a focal point of political, cultural, and historical significance for thousands of years. Located in the Middle East, Palestine is bordered by Egypt to the southwest, Jordan to the east, Syria to the north, and Lebanon to the northwest, with the Mediterranean Sea to the west.

History and Culture

Palestine has a rich and diverse history, with various empires and civilizations having ruled the region, including the ancient Canaanites, Philistines, Romans, Byzantines, and Ottomans. The region is home to numerous historical and cultural sites, including the Old City of Jerusalem, the Church of the Nativity in Bethlehem, and the Al-Aqsa Mosque compound.

Modern-Day Palestine

Today, Palestine is a disputed territory, with the Israeli-Palestinian conflict ongoing since the mid-20th century. The Palestinian National Authority (PNA) is recognized by the international community as the representative of the Palestinian people, and has been seeking statehood and self-determination since the 1990s.
